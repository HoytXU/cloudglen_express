Cloudglen Express

1. Value noise

The animation is generated entirely from a continuous random scalar field n: ℝ2 → [0, 1] sampled on a 1024×1024 lattice of independent uniform values g(p) stored in iChannel0. For an arbitrary point x ∈ ℝ2, let p = ⌊x⌋ and f = x − p. With the four corner values a = g(p), b = g(p + e1), c = g(p + e2), d = g(p + e1 + e2), the field is the bilinear interpolation

n(x) = a + (b−a) u(fx) + (c−a) u(fy) + (a−b−c+d) u(fx) u(fy),

where the interpolation parameter is the quintic fade

u(t) = 6t5 − 15t4 + 10t3.

By construction u(0) = 0, u(1) = 1, u'(0) = u'(1) = 0, and u''(0) = u''(1) = 0. The vanishing of u' eliminates the directional creases that occur on cell boundaries under bilinear interpolation; the additional vanishing of u'' eliminates the curvature creases that the cubic smoothstep 3t2−2t3 leaves visible after octave summation.

2. Fractal Brownian motion

Cloud silhouettes are produced by an octave-weighted sum of the noise field with doubling frequency:

fbm(x; α) = Σi=0N−1 αi n(2ix) / Σi=0N−1αi,   N = 8.

Two values of the gain α are used: α = 0.7 for the cloud silhouettes and α = 0.9 for the smoke. With lacunarity 2 and gain α the resulting power spectrum decays as f−log2α; gain 0.7 is close to pink noise, gain 0.9 closer to white. Normalising by Σαi keeps the output range independent of the octave count.

3. Layered horizon by height-field rasterization

The skyline is the union of thirteen horizontal cloud bands. Each band is parametrised by a midline m, a vertical amplitude D, a parallax distance d, and a phase offset φ. The displacement of its upper silhouette at column x is

h(x) = (fbm(x + (t/d + φ, 0); 8) − ½)·D,

where t = 4·iTime. A pixel at uv = (uvx, uvy) is filled with shade ci if

uvy < m + h(uvx) − δi,

where the per-shade offsets δi ∈ {0, 0.04, 0.08, 0.12, …} are tested in decreasing order. The first inequality satisfied returns its colour, so the offsets produce the inner gradient of a band, and processing bands from far (large d) to near produces the depth gradient. Because each band advects horizontally with rate 1/d, near bands move faster than far bands — classical two-dimensional parallax driven by a height field rather than textured strips.

4. Sub-frame foreground accumulation

For pixels in the lower half of the image (uvy < 0.5) the foreground colour is the temporal average

fg(uv) = (1/N) Σj=0N−1 F(uv, t + 4j/(60N)),   N = 5.

The five samples span 4(N−1)/(60N) ≈ 0.053 units in t, or about one display frame at 60 Hz. This band-limits the high-frequency aliasing produced by the step-based layer test on the fastest cloud bands by smearing each silhouette over a single frame's worth of motion.

5. Smoke plume

Let x = 0.49 − uvx denote horizontal distance behind the chimney, and let h(x) be the same fbm evaluated with gain 0.9 and a small reverse drift. The plume is the implicit region

| uvy + 0.4 h(x) − 0.16√x − 0.12 | < 0.8 x e−10x.

The centreline y0(x) = 0.16√x + 0.12 rises rapidly near the chimney and asymptotes; the half-thickness w(x) = 0.8 x e−10x is a Γ(2, 10)-shaped envelope peaking at x = 1/10; the additive 0.4 h(x) noise perturbs the centreline. A second test |·| < w(x)−0.02 shades a hollow core slightly darker.

6. Procedural train and bridge

The train is rasterised in fixed pixel-space coordinates by intersecting axis-aligned masks. Wagons replicate via the modular coordinate uv2 = fract(9·uv); a column is drawn whenever

step(0.05, 1 − |2 uv2,x − 1|) = 1,

i.e. when the modular coordinate lies within 0.05 of the cell centre. Wheels are squared-distance disks: a pixel belongs to the wheel of radius r centred at c iff

&lVert;uv − c&rVert;2 < r2,

implemented as 1 − step(r², dot(uv−c, uv−c)) to avoid the square root.

The bridge tiles three spans across the screen by uv2,x = fract(3 (uvx + t/5 + 32.5)). Its deck silhouette is the level set of the parabola

D(uv2) = uv2,y − 0.15 (uv2,x − ½)2 − 0.12.

The deck edge is rendered as smoothstep(0.001, 0.003, |D|), which approximates a sub-pixel anti-aliased isocontour. A higher-frequency modular coordinate fract(16·uv2,x) gates the X-truss pickets beneath the deck.

7. Vignette

The post pass multiplies the scene colour by

V(u, v) = ½ + ½ [16 u v (1−u) (1−v)]0.2.

The polynomial 16 u v (1−u) (1−v) is separable, equal to 1 at the centre (½, ½) and 0 on the borders. Raising it to the power 0.2 flattens the central plateau and concentrates the falloff near the edges; the affine envelope ½ + ½(·) caps the maximum darkening at 50 %, leaving the corners dimmed but not black.
